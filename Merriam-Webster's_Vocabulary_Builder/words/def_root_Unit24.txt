<p><strong>MAND</strong> means &quot;entrust&quot; or &quot;order.&quot; A <em>command</em> is an order; a <em>commandment</em> is also an order, but usually one that comes from God. And a <em>commando</em> unit carries out orders for special military actions.</p>
<p><strong>UND</strong> means &quot;wave&quot; and &quot;to rise in waves,&quot; &quot;to surge or flood.&quot; <em>Undulations</em> are waves or wavelike things or motions, and to <em>undulate</em> is to rise and fall in a wavelike way.</p>
<p><strong>SANCT</strong> meaning &quot;holy.&quot; Thus, <em>sanctity</em> means &quot;holiness.&quot; In ancient Greece, a spot could be <em>sanctified</em>, or "made holy," by a group of priests who carried out a solemn ritual; these might be spots where fumes arose from a crack in the earth or where a spring of clear water flowed out of the ground, and a temple might be built there for worship of a god.</p>
<p><strong>LOQU</strong> means &quot;to talk.&quot; An <em>eloquent</em> preacher</em> speaks fluently, forcefully, and expressively. And a dummy's words come out of a <em>ventriloquist</em>'s mouth--or perhaps out of his belly.</p>
<p><strong>VIR</strong> means &quot;man.&quot; A <em>virtue</em> is a good quality--originally, the kind of quality an ideal man possessed. And <em>virtuous</em> behavior is normally excellent. All in all, the Romans seem to have believed that being a man was a good thing.</p>
<p><strong>VAL</strong> meaning &quot;strength,&quot; and &quot;to be worthy, healthy, or strong&quot; and &quot;to have power or influence.&quot; So <em>evaluating</em> a house involves determining how healthy it is. A <em>valid</em> license or credit card is one that's still in effect, and a <em>valid</em> proof is one that provides strong evidence.</p>
<p><strong>CRE/CRET</strong> means &quot;to come into being&quot; and &quot;to grow.&quot; So a <em>crescendo</em> in music occurs when the music is growing louder, and a <em>decrescendo</em> when it's growing softer.</p>
<p><strong>FUS</strong> means &quot;to pour out&quot; or &quot;to melt.&quot; A <em>fuse</em> depends on melting metal to break an overloaded circuit. Nuclear <em>fusion</em> involves the &quot;melting&quot; together of light nuclei to form heavier nuclei, and <em>fusion</em> cuisine</em> brings together the cooking of two or more cultures.</p>
